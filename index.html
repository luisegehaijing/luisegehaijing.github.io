Luise Ge

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Washington University, St Louis, advised by two amazing scholar Yevgeniy Vorobeychik and Brendan Juba. I would like to describe myself as a generalist as my interests are generally very broad. I started out as a cognitive science student at Edinburgh, with the passion to figure out how mind works. While the curiosity has never been diminished, it just happened that the beauty of maths itself struck me. I ended up getting a master in mathematics with an emphasise in geometry and topology. My thesis advisor Paolo Cascini is one of the warmest professors I have encountered and I feel greateful to him greatly. I have come back to CS because I see many CS problems carrying meaning not only in the theoretical sphere but also in humanity. My research direction is so far still dynamic. In general I am interested in computational social choice, and the foundations of AI from learning, reasoning to decision making.

Project Page

We analyze the evaluation performance for each combination of agent count for training versus evaluation. We perform experimental evaluations on predator prey and traffic junction environments and demonstrate that it is possible to obtain similar or higher evaluation performance by training with less agents.
