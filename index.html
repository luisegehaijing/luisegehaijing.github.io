I am a Computer Science PhD student at Washington University, St Louis, advised by Yevgeniy Vorobeychik and Brendan Juba. My research direction is still quite dynamic. In general, I am interested in the theoretical foundations of AI ranging from learning theory to multi-agent system as well social choice and game theory. While these may all look abstract, it is both the philosophical implications of these problems and the potential help to the enviroment and society we live in that motivate me.
On a personal note, I would like to describe myself as a generalist as my interests are generally broad. I started out as a cognitive science student, with the passion to figure out how mind works. While this curiosity has never diminished, it just happened that the beauty of maths struck me greatly. I ended up getting a master in mathematics specialising in geometry and topology. My thesis advisor Paolo Cascini is one of the warmest professors I have encountered.
I always crave a deep conversation so please do not hesitate to reach out to me through my email.
